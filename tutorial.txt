##story##
Welcome to Slasha, a simple and easy to learn real time strategy game. Slasha doesn't have a setting or backstory - it's up to the individual campaigns to do that. This campaign will get you acquainted with the controls and basic strategy and get you fighting <insert enemy here> in no time! To control a unit, right click (or hit shift) next to it and left click (or enter/return) to tell it to move to a place. Hitting opponents is automated; you don't need to order your units to attack. Press P and try getting your unit close enough to kill the dummy. Note that you will start paused, this lets you take a look at the map and even tell units where to start moving to before you press P again to start.

##story##
Well done! Now, we'll introduce two more aspects of controlling your units: initial deployment and multiple unit selection. In a map with initial deployment turned on, you will be able to move your units anywhere within the deployment rectangle at startup. To select multiple units, simply hold down your right mouse button (or shift) and make a rectangle around the units. In initial deployment mode, you can left click (or enter) to position the units one at a time. In normal mode, setting a destination will set the same destination for all selected units, usually resulting in them crowding around the destination. Using the middle mouse button (or F) with multiple units selected will move them all but retain their formation. Try using these new controls and a strategy of fighting one unit at a time to win this battle (careful, the enemies bite now!)

##story##
In most levels, there is more than one type of unit. We've seen two of the main types already - soldiers and pikemen, the difference being that pikemen are weaker than soldiers in damage and health but are more offensively focused and have longer range. Thus, if you have an army of mixed soldiers and pikemen it is a good idea to put the soldiers in front and the pikemen behind them. Set up your army in this way and watch it obliterate this poorly organized enemy.  Note that in levels where you have a mixed group, your units will often come properly organized and ordered so that one-at-a-time placement in initial deployment will put the soldiers down first.
